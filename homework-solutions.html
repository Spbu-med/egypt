Problem 10: Drug Dissolution

Even with negative $\Delta S$, dissolution can occur if $\Delta H$ is sufficiently exothermic to make $\Delta G$ negative at certain temperatures.

Problem 11: Protein Denaturation

$Protein_{folded} \rightarrow Protein_{unfolded}$ $\implies \mathbf{\Delta S > 0}$
Thermodynamic Burn Impact: Increased $T$ makes $-T\Delta S$ highly negative, forcing the protein to unfold (denature) spontaneously.

Problem 12: NaCl Formation

At 298 K: $\Delta G = -822.2 - (298 \times -0.181) = \mathbf{-768.3\,kJ}$
Spontaneous at all Temps.

Problem 13: MnO2 Reduction

$\Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S$. Reaction is non-spontaneous at 298 K but becomes spontaneous at high industrial temperatures (1000 K).
